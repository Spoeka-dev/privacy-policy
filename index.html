02

Data Storage & Processing

We do not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on our own servers.

All data you enter is stored exclusively in one of the following places, both under your full control:

  • Locally on your device – Stored in the app's private SQLite database. This includes your shift patterns, nicknames, notes, salary settings, and earnings history.
  • System Calendar Access – To show your work shifts alongside your personal life, the app reads your device's system calendar. This data is processed locally only to display it in the app's calendar view; it is never stored by us or transmitted externally.
  • Public Holiday API – The app fetches holiday lists via the Nager.Date API. No personal data or device identifiers are sent; only the selected country/region and year are used to retrieve the data.

03

Google Drive Backup & AES-256 Encryption

The backup feature is manual and user-initiated. To protect your data, the app implements high-level security measures:

Encryption: Every backup is AES-256 encrypted in GCM mode on your device before it is uploaded to your personal Google Drive. This ensures that the content remains private and unreadable to anyone without your unique recovery key.

  • Recovery Key: You are solely responsible for the Recovery Key generated by the app. Because encryption happens locally, we do not have access to this key and cannot recover your data if the key is lost.
  • App-Specific Access: The app uses the appDataFolder scope. This means it can only see and manage the files it has created itself; it has no access to your other photos, documents, or data in Google Drive.
  • Revocation: You can revoke access at any time via your Google Account settings.

04

Legal Basis for Processing

Operations occur locally on your device or within your own cloud account. The legal basis for the optional backup is your consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR), which you give by initiating the backup process.

If you contact us for support, we process your contact details based on Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (Legitimate Interest) and delete them once the matter is resolved.

05

Permissions & Third Parties

  • Google Play Store: Technical metadata (install stats, device type) is handled by Google under their privacy policy.
  • Calendar Permission: Required to display system events. This data never leaves the device.
  • Exact Alarm Permission: Used solely to trigger your shift reminders accurately at the requested time.
